## Teleprompter script

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 2 — talking head, medium.]

Claude Code is not a chatbot. It is the same model you might already use in a browser, but it has a different job. In a browser, Claude is a very smart person on a phone call. You describe your files. They give you advice. You copy the advice back into your work.

[PAUSE — 1s beat]

Claude Code is that same person sitting at your desk. They can see your folder. They can open a file. They can make the edit for you. And when they finish, the change is on disk, not in a chat log.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 3 — overlay graphic. Two columns. Left: "Chatbot." Right: "Agent in the room." Pen-line icons.]

That is the whole mental model. Everything else in this course is detail.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 4 — talking head.]

The analogy I want you to hold in your head is a careful junior colleague. Not a senior engineer who takes the problem and disappears for a week. Not a search engine that gives you ten links. A careful, competent, slightly cautious junior colleague. You tell them what to do. They tell you what they are about to do. You say yes, or you say no, or you redirect. They do the work. You check it.

[PAUSE — 1s beat]

That is the shape of every session.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 5 — animated diagram. A folder icon labeled "Your project." Inside: three file icons. An agent icon on the side. An arrow labeled "reads" points from the agent into the folder. A second arrow labeled "writes (with your approval)" points back.]

Here is what that looks like in practice. You open a terminal. You move into a folder. You run one command. The agent now knows the shape of that folder. It can see what files are there. It can read any of them. When you ask it to do something, it can suggest an edit, run a command, summarize, or ask you a question.

[B-ROLL: slow pan across a few file icons. No narration.]

[PAUSE — 1s beat]

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 6 — overlay graphic. Four pen-line icons in a row. Labels under each: "Reads files," "Writes files (with approval)," "Runs commands (with approval)," "Asks you questions."]

Everything the agent can do falls into four buckets. It can read your files. It can write to your files, if you say yes. It can run commands on your computer, if you say yes. And it can ask you a question when it is unsure.

The two "if you say yes" items are the whole safety story. By default, Claude Code does not write anything or run anything without asking. When it wants to do either, it stops and shows you what it is about to do.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 7 — still frame of a terminal. An approve-or-deny prompt is visible. A camel-colored arrow points at it.]

[CURSOR HIGHLIGHT: the approve dialog area]

This is the moment you have control. A real edit, about to happen, and you get to read it first. In a later video we will watch this approval dance in detail. For now, just know that the agent is not a runaway.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 8 — talking head.]

Why does any of this matter. Because the thing most people try first with AI is the wrong thing. They paste a whole document into a chatbot. They get a long answer. They copy it back out. They discover three hours later that they lost their formatting, or their tracked changes, or the second half of the file. The loop is slow and the file never really leaves your hands.

[PAUSE — 1s beat]

Claude Code changes the loop. The agent meets the file where it lives. You do not paste. You do not copy back. You ask, you approve, and the work shows up where you expected it.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 9 — diagram: "When to use which." Two columns again. Left column "Browser chatbot": "Quick questions." "Thinking out loud." "No file involved." "You want advice, not action." Right column "Claude Code": "You have a folder of stuff." "You want the edit on disk." "You want a command run." "You are doing the work, not theorizing about it."]

The two tools are not rivals. They are different instruments. A good rule: if there is a folder involved, you probably want Claude Code. If there is no folder involved, the browser is fine.

[B-ROLL: slow cross-fade between the two column graphics. No narration, 2 seconds.]

[PAUSE — 1s beat]

One more thing before we stop. Some people hear "agent in the room" and get nervous. They picture an autonomous robot touching everything on their hard drive. That is not the shape of this tool. The agent lives in the one folder you open it in. It does not wander. It does not read your email. It does not know about your other projects unless you point it there on purpose. The scope is the folder. That is one of the things we are going to make sure you get right from day one.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 10 — talking head with an end card in the corner. The card reads: "Next: Install."]

[PAUSE — 2s beat]

So that is the model. A careful junior colleague. In the folder. Reads, writes with approval, runs with approval, asks. Everything else builds on that.
