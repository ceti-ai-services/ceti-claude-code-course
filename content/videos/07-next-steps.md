## Teleprompter script

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 2 — talking head.]

Let me recap. Bronze covered six things.

One. The mental model. Claude Code is not a chatbot. It is a careful junior colleague in your folder.

Two. Installation. Node, one npm command, one word to launch.

Three. A first session. Move into a folder. Ask a question. Get a useful answer about real files.

Four. File permissions. Backup first. Read the diff. Approve or deny.

Five. CLAUDE dot MD. Teach Claude who you are, what this folder is, and how you want it to write. Once. Every session after that starts with context.

Six. One real use case. Look, plan, execute. The pattern that works for almost every job you will throw at this tool.

[PAUSE — 1s beat]

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 3 — overlay graphic: six pen-line icons in a 2x3 grid. Labels underneath: "Mental model," "Install," "First session," "Permissions," "CLAUDE.md," "Real use case."]

Those six ideas are the kit. Most professionals do not get past this. They install, they poke around, they get bored, they quit. You are not going to do that, because you have a plan.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 4 — talking head.]

Here is the plan for your first week.

[STAGE DIRECTION: Shot 5 — overlay graphic: checklist with four items.]

One. Pick three folders on your machine that you actually work in. Not your whole hard drive. Three folders with real stuff. For each of those folders, write a short CLAUDE dot MD. Two sentences per section is fine. Start simple.

Two. Run the "what is in this folder" question on each of them. See how Claude summarizes your actual work. This is calibration. You are learning what it gets right and what it gets wrong.

Three. Pick one task you were going to do manually this week. Could be cleaning files. Could be summarizing a document. Could be turning notes into an outline. Run it through look, plan, execute. Watch yourself save twenty minutes.

Four. Keep the backup habit. Before any edit on a file you care about, `cp` the file to a dot bak. Every time. Until it is muscle memory.

Silver covers three things Bronze does not.

[CURSOR HIGHLIGHT: row 2]

[CURSOR HIGHLIGHT: row 3]

Hooks. These are small rules that fire automatically when Claude does certain things. Want Claude to always run your linter after editing a file. Hook. Want Claude to never touch a specific folder. Hook. Want a specific notification on a specific event. Hook. You write three of them during week three.

[CURSOR HIGHLIGHT: row 4]

And a capstone. Every Silver student ships one real project by week four. Past capstones: a personal research system, an automated reporting pipeline, a custom document-drafting workflow for a law firm, a sales call note processor. Your capstone will be something that actually matters in your work.
