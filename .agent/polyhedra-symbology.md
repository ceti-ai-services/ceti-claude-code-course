# Polyhedra Symbology — pedagogical reference

The symbolic root for the 8 shapes in `<Polyhedron>` and the module mapping in `useModuleGlyph.ts`. Three lineages converge: **Platonic** (the element), **Fuller/Synergetics** (what the shape does in Bucky's geometry), and **Pedagogical** (what concept it teaches best).

Shapes repeat across modules so readers build a vocabulary: *when I see this shape again, I know what kind of moment I'm in.*

---

## 1. Tetrahedron — 4 triangular faces

- **Geometry.** The minimum enclosed volume in 3D space. Fewer faces = no interior.
- **Platonic.** Fire. Sharpest, most angular — ignition, beginning, the spark.
- **Fuller.** *The* foundational unit of synergetics. Volume is counted in tetrahedra.
- **Teaches.** Firsts. Atomic, irreducible ideas. The single concept a module begins with.

## 2. Hexahedron (Cube) — 6 square faces

- **Geometry.** The only Platonic with square faces. Tessellates space. Stable on any face.
- **Platonic.** Earth. Ground, walls, the immovable.
- **Fuller.** *Derived*, not fundamental — a cube is 3 tetrahedra wearing a costume. Its role is **enclosure**, not generation.
- **Teaches.** Stability, boundaries, installation, gates. Laying a foundation or enforcing rules.

## 3. Octahedron — 8 triangular faces

- **Geometry.** Two square-base pyramids joined at the base. The **dual** of the cube.
- **Platonic.** Air. The medium of exchange, breath, the between.
- **Fuller.** Two tetrahedra apex-to-apex — the **dialogue** shape. Up-tetra vs. down-tetra in equilibrium.
- **Teaches.** Duality. Two things meeting. Approve/deny. Think/act. Read/write.

## 4. Dodecahedron — 12 pentagonal faces

- **Geometry.** 12 regular pentagons. The only Platonic with five-fold symmetry. Cannot tile 3D — it points beyond itself.
- **Platonic.** Aether / cosmos. Plato's "shape the Demiurge used to arrange the constellations."
- **Fuller.** Sits at the outer shell of nested polyhedra; holds the whole system.
- **Teaches.** Wholeness, containers, the cosmos of a thing. The place everything else lives.

## 5. Icosahedron — 20 triangular faces

- **Geometry.** 20 faces, 12 vertices, 30 edges. Most sphere-like Platonic solid.
- **Platonic.** Water. Flow, membrane, the shape that rolls.
- **Fuller.** A tensegrity favorite — 12 vertices as connection nodes. Geodesic domes collapse into it.
- **Teaches.** Flow, networks, membranes with many contact points.

## 6. Cuboctahedron — 14 faces (8 triangles + 6 squares)

- **Geometry.** An Archimedean solid. 12 identical vertices, each equidistant from center. Tetra and octa wearing each other.
- **Fuller.** Renamed the **Vector Equilibrium** — the only polyhedron where center-to-vertex equals edge length. All forces balance. Zero-point of synergetics.
- **Teaches.** Balance. A working real-world task with many forces in proportion.

## 7. Tetrahelix — helical chain of tetrahedra

- **Geometry.** Not Platonic. A chain of face-joined tetrahedra twisting around an axis. Irrational pitch — never returns to the same position.
- **Fuller.** Observed in DNA, phyllotaxis, kelp. Growth with **minimum structural change** — each new tetrahedron reuses the last face.
- **Teaches.** Compounding reuse, helical growth. Something that pays returns with repetition.

## 8. Vector Equilibrium (VE) — cuboctahedron + 12 radii

- **Geometry.** The cuboctahedron *plus* its 12 radii-to-center as vectors, plus a reference sphere.
- **Fuller.** The most important shape in synergetics: "the zero-starting-point for happenings or non-happenings." The diagram of how all forces balance.
- **Teaches.** Packaged wholeness. A capability held ready, dispatched from center. The cuboctahedron *labeled* as a working system.

## Shape reuse as vocabulary

A symbol that appears once is noise. A symbol that appears 2–3 times is a word. The mapping targets **5–6 distinct shapes across 14 modules**, each reappearing where its meaning re-applies:

- **Tetrahedron** — *firsts* — M01.
- **Hexahedron** — *foundation / gate* — M02, M10.
- **Octahedron** — *duality / exchange* — M03, M09.
- **Dodecahedron** — *the container / cosmos* — M05, M14.
- **Icosahedron** — *membrane with many contact points* — M04, M11.
- **Cuboctahedron** — *balanced real task* (intentional singleton) — M06.
- **Tetrahelix** — *compounding reuse* — M07, M12.
- **Vector Equilibrium** — *packaged working wholeness* — M08, M13.

When the reader sees the icosahedron twice — once for permissions, once for MCP — the rhyme should land: *both are membranes with many contact points between me and Claude.* That rhyme is the pedagogy.

The cuboctahedron singleton is deliberate: M06 (one real task, end-to-end) is the course's only "equilibrium of a deliverable" moment. It rhymes with VE (M08, M13) through shared geometry but keeps its distinct role — the *un-labeled* balance, the work itself rather than the system around it.
